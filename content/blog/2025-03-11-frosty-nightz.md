**Introducing Frosty Nightz, a semi-regular jam session in the 1974 time machine that is my basement.** **The idea: Get musical people together, eat, drink, play music, and have fun.** That's it. That's the whole thing. No bigger project. No ulterior motives. Just an excuse to connect with two of most important things life has to offer: People and Music. ![](https://bradfrost.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/PXL_20250309_010849851.MP_-1024x576.jpg)Getting set up for some jamming. A stready trickle of musicians continued down the basement stairs to join the session. Frosty Nightz is the spiritual successor to the practices leading up to [Frostapalooza](https://frostapalooza.bradfrost.com/). In the year leading up to our show in August, [we had monthly practices at our house](https://bradfrost.com/blog/post/frostapalooza-one-epic-night/). Those practices were so much fun:

I needed a few months to decompress after the show, but intended to set this up in the early winter. Alas, life got in the way, and it was a scramble as I suddenly [entered a new phase of my career](https://bradfrost.com/blog/post/announcement-im-going-independent/).  Now that I'm back on my feet, I planted the calendar-invite flag in the distance and let all of the Pittsburgh-based Frostapalooza musicians know about it. Since Frostapalooza, I met even more amazing humans/musicians through [AcoustiCafe](https://www.acousticafe.org/) at Mr Smalls and at the ultra-fun [Pittsburgh Plays](https://www.pittsburghplays.com/) The Cure show in December. So I extended the invite to those folks as well. Frosty Nightz, Volume 1 On Saturday night, people came over and we got to work. Ian, Melissa, and I were joined by [Zachary Lee](https://soundcloud.com/realzacharylee), Amber Johnson, Harper Lines, and "The Steves" from [Phase Shifter Shifter](https://soundcloud.com/phase-shifter-shifter), [Bethany Conley](https://www.instagram.com/bb_conley/) and her partner. I had a vague sense of everyone's musical talents ahead of time, but I was surprised and delighted at everyone's willingness to just Go For It. There was no real prompting or much discussion; we just played music. Everything was done on the fly, songs were created in real-time. Harper and Bethany provided vocals that provided a lot of welcome guidance, and Zachary and The Steves brought so much of their improvisational prowess to the equation. There's a magical part of the _Get Back_ documentary where we get to be a fly on the wall as Paul McCartney [iterates his way into writing the song _Get Back_](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X94t4hTajCc).
